During the 2025 Circular Economy Conference held in Barcelona, over 500 professionals gathered to share experiences, challenges, and opportunities around sustainability and innovation.The sessions focused on key topics such as eco-design, material reuse, and the creation of more responsible business models. International experts presented real-life case studies with promising results.

“Circular economy is not just an option, it’s an urgent necessity,” stated Dounia Wone, one of the keynote speakers.

In addition to talks, hands-on workshops allowed attendees to learn methodologies they could apply to their own projects. These spaces encouraged collaboration among companies, institutions, and startups.

The event concluded with the presentation of a joint manifesto to promote public policies aligned with circularity principles. Organizers committed to continuing this work annually.

The session, organized by Lúcid, EIG, and D!OS, aimed to share knowledge with manufacturing companies and guide them in the mindset needed to embrace a conceptual shift in the way they design and market their products.

The new action plan announces initiatives along the entire life cycle of products. It targets how products are designed, promotes circular economy processes, encourages sustainable consumption, and aims to ensure that waste is prevented and the resources used are kept in the EU economy for as long as possible.
